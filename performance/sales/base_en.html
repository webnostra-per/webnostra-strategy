“Phuket is Thailand's largest tourist island. Every year, over 10 million visitors come here. Because land is limited and demand stays high, property prices have been consistently rising.”

⏱ The First 30 Seconds Critical

▼

Objective: take control and set the frame.

Script
“Hi John, you submitted an inquiry about a villa in Phuket. Do you have 3–4 minutes? I'll ask a couple of quick questions to understand what you're looking for, and then I can suggest some specific options.”

What we accomplish here:

  • Stated the purpose of the call
  • Set a time frame (3–4 minutes)
  • Got a micro-commitment

🔍 Qualification 4 Parameters

▼

Qualification is not an interrogation — it is steering the conversation. 4 key parameters:

  1. Purpose Investment or personal use?
  2. Budget What price range are you considering?
  3. Timeline When are you planning to buy?
  4. Location Do you have a specific area in mind, or are you open to options?
Rule
Never send a proposal until all four parameters are understood.

🛡 Three Trust Factors Mandatory

▼

If any one of these is missing, the deal will not close.

1. Trust in the Property
“This project has already gained +30% in value during the construction phase.”
2. Trust in the Company
“We are a top-6 agency by sales volume — over 50 deals closed this year.”
3. Trust in the Manager
“I personally handle every deal from selection through to registration.”

🔇 Re-engaging Ghost Leads Win-Back

▼

When a lead goes silent, don't just “follow up again.” Use an interest hook to re-engage:

Script
“Hi John, just wanted to check — are you still considering a purchase, or have you put things on hold for now? I ask because the installment plan for this project ends this week.”
Principle
We don't pressure. We bring them back onto the decision line.

💻 Closing for a Zoom Call Key Stage

▼

In real estate, you don't close deals in a chat. The goal of the first call is to get them onto Zoom.

Script
“John, rather than guessing and sending you dozens of listings, let me suggest a 20-minute Zoom call — I'll show you 3 options that match your budget and walk you through the projected returns.”

⚠ Common Mistakes & Tone Prohibited

▼

Strictly prohibited:

  • Sending a proposal without qualifying the lead
  • Failing to ask about budget
  • Not establishing your expertise
  • Giving up after 3 follow-ups
  • Not pushing for a Zoom call
Tone Management
In real estate, what you are selling is: confidence, stability, expertise, and composure. Never sound rushed or apologetic.

Closing Scripts for Reservation

A step-by-step closing sequence for investors — through decision logic, not pressure

Core Principle
An investor makes a decision when they: understand the returns, trust the project, trust the manager, and sense the risk of missing out.

10 Steps to Close

▼
  1. Confirm Interest Make sure the property is the right fit.
    “John, am I right that out of all the options, this project resonated with you the most?”
  2. Recap Key Benefits Briefly remind them why this property makes sense: location, price trajectory, rental yield.
  3. Surface Remaining Doubts Clear any last objections before the reservation.
    “Is there anything that's giving you pause at this point?”
  4. Build a Logical Case for Commitment Guide them toward the idea of locking in the price.
    “The current price is lower than it will be after the next increase. Investors typically lock in their unit at the earliest stage possible.”
  5. Explain the Reservation Process Keep the explanation as simple as possible.
    “A reservation holds a specific unit for you. The property is taken off the market and the current price is locked in.”
  6. Reduce Perceived Risk The investor needs to know the reservation is safe.
    “If you change your mind, the reservation deposit can be refunded or transferred to a different unit.”
  7. Make the Reservation Offer A direct offer after logical groundwork.
    “If this property is the right fit, we can lock it in with a reservation.”
  8. Create Urgency Show that the unit could be taken by someone else.
    “There are only two units with this layout still available.”
  9. Simplify the Decision Make the process feel effortless.
    “The reservation takes just a few minutes. I'll send you the form and the payment details.”
  10. Final Close A soft closing question.
    “Shall we lock in this unit for you?”

Psychological Triggers

10 techniques that accelerate the buying decision in real estate

Most Powerful Combination
Price Escalation + Scarcity + Social Proof + Expert Trust = decision speed multiplied several times over.
01
Scarcity
When supply is limited, decisions happen faster. The brain interprets scarcity as value.
“There are only 5 units left at the current price in this project.”
02
Price Escalation
Investors fear missing out on growth. Show the price trajectory across construction stages.
“At launch — $160K. Now — $195K. After completion — $230K.”
03
Early Entry
The earliest stage offers the greatest upside. Walk them through the stages: pre-sale → construction → completion.
The investor needs to understand where maximum profit lies.
04
Social Proof
People feel safer when they see others have already invested. Share real transactions and case studies.
“Over 70% of this project has already been sold to European investors.”
05
Authority
Buyers decide faster when they trust the expert. The manager speaks as a consultant, not a salesperson.
Expertise, company track record, developer partnerships.
06
Safety
The investor must feel that risks are minimized: developer reputation, legal structure, guarantees.
Reduces the fear of losing money.
07
Simplicity
If the deal looks complicated, the decision gets delayed. Explain the process and show support at every stage.
A straightforward buying process + property management included.
08
Passive Income
Investors respond to clear, quantified income. Provide specific numbers: rental yield, payback period.
“Projected rental yield of 8–10% per year.”
09
Comparison
Investors always compare. Help them: bank deposit vs. real estate, different markets side by side.
“A bank deposit gives you 4%. Rental income on this property — 9%.”
10
Fear of Missing Out
An extremely powerful trigger. Investors regret properties they didn't buy. Show price growth from past projects.
“In the previous project, prices went up 35% in 2 years.”

Sales Department Standard Operating Procedures

Segment: Real Estate (Premium / Investment)

⚡ Response Time 15 Minutes

▼
  • Respond to every inbound lead within 15 minutes during business hours
  • For leads received after hours — respond within 15 minutes of the start of the next business day
  • Every missed call = a mandatory follow-up message via WhatsApp
Warning
Slow response times reduce conversion rates by 30–50%.

📞 Follow-Up Cadence 7+ Touchpoints

▼
Day 1
Call → WhatsApp message → Log in CRM
Day 2
Call → Message
Day 3
Call → Message
Day 7
Call → Message
Day 14
Call → Message
Rule
A minimum of 7 touchpoints before closing a lead. Giving up after 2–3 attempts is strictly prohibited.

🔍 Qualification Mandatory

▼

The manager must gather this information before sending any proposal:

  1. Purpose Investment or personal use
  2. Budget Price range
  3. Timeline When they plan to buy
  4. Location Area of interest
  5. Source of Funds Where the money is coming from
Prohibited
Sending a proposal without this information is strictly prohibited.

💾 CRM Discipline Discipline

▼
  • Every touchpoint is logged in the CRM
  • A follow-up task must be created after every interaction
  • Overdue tasks are unacceptable
  • Lead records must be fully completed: budget, purpose, timeline, location, status
Principle
The CRM is a management tool, not a formality.

💻 Transition to Zoom ×2–3 Conversion

▼

The goal of the first contact is to get the lead onto Zoom. Zoom call structure:

  1. Identify the buyer's objective
  2. Present 2–3 relevant properties
  3. Financial model ROI, projected returns
  4. Handle objections
  5. Close on a reservation / hold
Fact
Without a Zoom call, conversion drops by 2–3x.

🚫 Prohibited Actions Strict

▼
  • Sending a proposal without qualification
  • Closing a lead after only 2–3 follow-ups
  • Leaving tasks overdue
  • Working outside the CRM
  • Responding hours later

📊 Feedback to Marketing Daily

▼

Every manager is required to log the following daily:

  • Qualified / not qualified
  • Reason for disqualification
  • Buyer's country
  • Budget
Why
Without this feedback, marketing cannot optimize the ad campaigns.
